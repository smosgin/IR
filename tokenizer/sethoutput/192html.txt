us state department institutes policy change toward ethiopian press
during christopher146s visit to addis ababa

policy change enacted after review of cpj report revealing more journalists imprisoned in ethiopia in past three years than in any other african country


new yorkï¿½ us secretary of state warren christopher today declined the ethiopian government146s invitation to hold a joint press conference upon his arrival in addis ababa this in response to a report by the new yorkbased committee to protect journalists cpj detailing the legal harassment and imprisonment of ethiopian journalists and the government146s systematic exclusion of the private press from official press conferences state department spokesperson nicholas burns told the committee146s chair kati marton that cpj146s report clampdown in addis ethiopia146s journalists at risk led to the policy change

in a public statement secretary of state christopher told reporters 147ethiopia has made progress in human rights during the past five years but the united states wants to see more  one of the areas of our concern is the freedom of the press and the treatment of journalists148 

cpj146s report is based on a factfinding mission conducted last may by africa program coordinator kakuna kerina who wrote the report and cpj board member and newsday un bureau chief josh friedman who wrote the report146s introductory essay based on more than 50 interviews with government officials journalists and other sources the report demonstrates how independent journalists are regularly harassed censored and jailed under the provisions of a restrictive press law enacted in 1992 the repressive climate exists despite promises of press freedom made by the transitional government of ethiopia tge in 1991 and by the recently elected prime minister meles zenawi who served as tge president clampdown in addis which was released on october 7  the day ethiopia146s parliament reconvened to address key media issues  made several recommendations to the meles administration and called on the united states and other western countries to take a more aggressive role in encouraging the ethiopian government to uphold guarantees of press freedom

147secretary of state christopher146s position is a welcome step toward what cpj hopes will be a continued us government commitment to press freedom and support for ethiopia146s independent journalists148 said kerina  147we look forward to the time when the ethiopian government views the private press as an ally not an enemy in the democratic process148
